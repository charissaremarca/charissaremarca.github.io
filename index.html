Case Study

A documented example of operational problem-solving in a SaaS support environment.

IAM · SSO · ESCALATION MANAGEMENT

Resolving SSO Escalation Overload & Restoring SLA Compliance at Scale

C Challenge

A sustained spike in SSO configuration failures and third-party integration breakages drove ticket volume well beyond normal Tier 1–2 capacity. Enterprise customers were experiencing delayed provisioning, failed SCIM syncs, and SAML attribute mismatches — each generating multiple escalations. Documentation was fragmented, troubleshooting paths were inconsistent, and escalation routing to engineering had no standardized format, creating repeated back-and-forth and stalled resolutions.

A Action
  • Diagnosed root pattern: Identified that the majority of SSO-related tickets stemmed from three recurring gaps — incorrect ACS URLs, missing attribute mappings, and misconfigured SCIM base URLs. Flagged as a systemic documentation gap, not isolated incidents.
  • Rebuilt the documentation structure: Rewrote SSO and SCIM troubleshooting runbooks from scratch with step-by-step decision trees, common error states, and verified resolution paths for each identity provider (Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace).
  • Standardized escalation format: Created a structured Jira handoff template including environment context (Prod vs. QA), steps already attempted, logs attached, and severity classification — eliminating unnecessary back-and-forth.
  • Coordinated with engineering: Established a recurring async sync channel for active escalations, enabling faster triage of recurring bug patterns and cleaner reproduction steps.
  • Proactive customer communication: Drafted status update templates for enterprise accounts during active incidents, reducing inbound escalation noise by setting clear resolution timelines.
R Result
100% SLA Compliance maintained throughout spike period
↓ 40% Reduction in repeat SSO-related escalations post-documentation
↑ 95%+ CSAT maintained across all resolved enterprise tickets
3× Faster engineering escalation resolution with structured handoff

The runbooks and escalation framework created during this period became standard operating procedure for the team, improving resolution consistency and reducing ticket reopen rates.
